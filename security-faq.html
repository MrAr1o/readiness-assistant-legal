Security & Privacy FAQ — Readiness Assistant

*Public-facing. Last updated: June 2026.*

This page answers the most common security and privacy questions about how Readiness Assistant works, what data it accesses, and how that data is handled.

For vulnerability reports, contact: support@steiktech.de

For privacy questions, contact: support@steiktech.de

General

What does this app actually do?

Readiness Assistant scans your Confluence Cloud spaces to help you prepare for audits, certifications, and enterprise customer reviews. It identifies documentation gaps, stale pages, ownerless pages, and missing controls — and generates a readiness score and gap checklist.

It does not certify compliance, issue audit opinions, or provide legal advice.

Is this a compliance certification tool?

No. This app is a documentation visibility and readiness workflow tool. It helps you see what evidence exists in Confluence, what is missing, and what needs to be fixed before an audit.

A readiness score or "FOUND" status does not mean your organisation is compliant with any regulation or standard. All outputs are advisory only. Formal compliance determinations require qualified legal, audit, or compliance professionals.

Does the app provide legal advice?

No. Nothing produced by this app — including gap reports, readiness scores, control mappings, or Audit Pack exports — constitutes legal advice, regulatory guidance, or audit assurance.

Data Access

What Confluence data does the app read?

The app reads:

  • Page titles and body text — to scan for evidence keywords that map to compliance controls, and to detect sensitive data patterns (if that feature is enabled)
  • Page metadata — last-modified dates and author information, to detect stale and ownerless pages
  • Space names and keys — to list available spaces and apply filtering

The app uses four Atlassian API scopes: read:page:confluence, read:space:confluence, read:confluence-user, and storage:app.

Can the app write to or modify Confluence pages?

No. The app requests read-only access to Confluence content. It cannot create, edit, move, or delete any Confluence pages, spaces, or user accounts.

Does the app access all spaces automatically?

The app scans the Confluence spaces accessible to the installing user's permissions. Admins can configure space exclusions in Settings to prevent specific spaces from being scanned. Individual page exclusions are also supported.

Data Storage

Does the app store raw Confluence page content?

No. Page body text is loaded into memory during a scan, processed, and then discarded. The raw text is never written to storage.

What is stored instead: references to pages (title, URL, space key), matched keywords, and derived findings (e.g. "GDPR-1: privacy policy documentation — MISSING").

Where is data stored?

All data is stored in Atlassian Forge Storage — a per-installation, isolated key-value store that is part of Atlassian's infrastructure. Each app installation has its own isolated storage namespace. No data is stored on SteikTech's own servers or external databases.

Does the app send data outside Atlassian?

No. Version 1 makes no calls to any external service.

All processing runs within the Atlassian Forge runtime. There are no calls to:

  • External AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.)
  • Third-party analytics services
  • External databases or APIs
  • Any SteikTech-operated servers

Does the app use external AI or LLMs?

No, not in version 1. All analysis is deterministic, keyword-heuristic analysis — no machine learning, no external AI API calls.

If a future version introduces optional AI features, this will be clearly disclosed and opt-in.

Data Retention and Deletion

How long is data retained?

  • Compliance and health findings: latest scan retained, overwritten by subsequent scans
  • Scan summaries: last 30 scans retained
  • Workflow records: until manually deleted or the app is uninstalled
  • App settings: until reset or uninstalled

What happens when the app is uninstalled?

App data stored in Atlassian Forge Storage is managed by the Atlassian Forge platform for that installation. SteikTech does not retain a separate copy or backup on its own systems.

How can admins delete stored data without uninstalling?

Admins can use the "Reset Data" function in the app's Settings panel to clear all stored scan findings, scores, and history.

For deletion of specific workflow records, use the workflow record management interface within the app.

Sensitive Data Detection

What is the Sensitive Data Check feature?

The Sensitive Data Check (called "Data Safety") is an optional, secondary feature that is disabled by default. When enabled by a Confluence admin:

  • The app scans page text for common credential patterns (API keys, tokens, connection strings, PII patterns — over 30 pattern types)
  • When a match is found, a redacted preview is stored (e.g. sk_live_51H...xQ) — the original value is discarded immediately
  • No credential or secret is ever stored in plain text

Does the app ever store secrets or credentials in plain text?

No. The Sensitive Data Check detects patterns and immediately creates a redacted preview. The original matched value is never written to storage.

Permissions

Why does the app need read:page:confluence?

To scan pages for compliance evidence. The app reads page text to detect whether documentation matching each compliance control exists.

Why does the app need read:space:confluence?

To list spaces so users can see which spaces are scanned, and to apply space inclusion/exclusion configuration.

Why does the app need read:confluence-user?

To detect ownerless pages. The app reads the author/owner linked to each page and surfaces pages with no identifiable owner.

Why does the app need storage:app?

To persist scan findings, readiness scores, workflow records, and settings between sessions. Without storage, the app would need to re-scan the entire workspace every time the dashboard is opened.

Data Residency and Infrastructure

Does the app support data residency?

The app stores data in Atlassian Forge Storage. The physical data residency location is determined by Atlassian's infrastructure and your organisation's Atlassian subscription agreement. The app does not independently control data residency.

If data residency location is a contractual or regulatory requirement for your organisation, consult Atlassian's documentation on Forge Storage data residency.

Does the app participate in Atlassian's "Runs on Atlassian" program?

The app is built entirely on Atlassian Forge and uses only Atlassian-provided infrastructure. Eligibility for the Runs on Atlassian badge is subject to formal Atlassian Marketplace review.

⚠️ Verify "Runs on Atlassian" eligibility in the Atlassian Marketplace Partner Portal.

Where is the app infrastructure hosted?

The app has no independently-hosted infrastructure. It runs entirely as an Atlassian Forge function — Atlassian's serverless platform for Marketplace apps.

Vulnerability Handling

How does the app handle security vulnerabilities?

We maintain a responsible disclosure process. If you discover a security vulnerability:

  1. Report it to support@steiktech.de
  2. We acknowledge within 48 hours
  3. We provide initial assessment within 7 days
  4. Critical issues: target remediation within 30 days

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who act in good faith, report responsibly, and do not access data beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the issue.

See our full Vulnerability Disclosure Policy: [SECURITY_DISCLOSURE.md]

Does the app have any compliance certifications?

The app itself is not certified against ISO 27001, SOC 2, or other standards. It runs on Atlassian Forge infrastructure; Atlassian's own compliance certifications apply to the underlying platform.
